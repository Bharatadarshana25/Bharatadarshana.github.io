India-Pakistan Wars

The history between India and Pakistan has been marked by multiple wars, primarily over the Kashmir region, starting in 1947-48, followed by conflicts in 1965, 1971, and the Kargil War in 1999.

Source: Wikipedia - Indo-Pakistani wars and conflicts

Who Betrayed India in 1947?

The partition of India in 1947 led to immense turmoil. The hurried and poorly planned partition by the British, combined with leaders like Muhammad Ali Jinnah pushing for Pakistan, is seen by many as a betrayal that led to mass displacement and violence.

Source: Britannica - Partition of India

Why the War Started Between India and Pakistan?

The wars primarily started due to the dispute over Kashmir, a princely state with a Muslim majority but ruled by a Hindu king at the time of independence, which led to conflicting claims and military action from both sides.

Source: Council on Foreign Relations - India-Pakistan Conflict

Who Supports Pakistan?

Pakistan has historically received support from countries like China, the United States (at different periods), and Saudi Arabia, for strategic, military, and economic reasons, impacting the geopolitics of South Asia.

Source: Brookings - US Relations with Pakistan

Why Pakistan Lies So Much

Pakistan's government and media have been accused by various sources of misinformation, denial of involvement in terrorism, and propaganda to influence international opinion.

Source: DW - Why Pakistan denies terrorism support
